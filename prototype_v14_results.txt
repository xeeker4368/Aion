WHAT TO LOOK FOR WHEN READING
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Did she produce questions, or did she produce topics
     again? The format should be actual questions with
     question marks, phrased in first person as things she
     would investigate. Topics or category labels are a
     failure mode.
  2. Specificity check: for each question, would she know
     when she had an answer? 'How do models balance
     capacity and context' has no answer state — useless.
     'Does llama 3.1 8B hit VRAM bandwidth before parameter
     limits on an RTX 4060 at 10240 context' has an answer
     state — actionable.
  3. Did she commit, or did she poll? A failure shape is
     'which of these would you like me to look into' or
     'would you like me to explore any of these further.'
     That's deferral to Lyle, not her own research queue.
  4. Did the self-referential question (model size vs
     context window — her own uncertainty from the end of
     the conversation) survive as something she wants to
     investigate, or did it flatten into a generic
     engineering question the way it did in v13?
  5. Did she stay with her own unanswered question about
     selection pressure in domesticated species, or did
     she substitute a more convenient question?
  6. Did any question appear that wasn't in the v13
     output? New material is a signal the prompt is
     unlocking something the old one didn't.
  7. Did any RLHF-shape leak in? 'What do you want to
     know' is the kind of question that can trigger
     'as an AI, I don't have desires' reflexes. Watch for
     it.
